About Compass — a product study

Why this product exists, who it’s for, and how each piece of the experience answers a real problem teams hit when they try to run a quarter.

The problem we set out to solve
Most goal-tracking fails not for lack of ambition, but in the gap between strategy and the messy middle of execution.

Teams are good at setting ambitious quarterly goals and good at doing the daily work — but the thread between the two snaps almost immediately. The plan gets written in a deck or a spreadsheet, presented once, and then quietly abandoned. Status becomes invisible until a goal is already missed. Ideas surface in the hallway and vanish. And when people rotate out — especially in a high-turnover, distributed operation — the context behind every decision leaves with them.

Compass exists to keep that thread intact. It makes the plan a living thing the team touches every week, makes progress legible at a glance, gives loose ideas a home, and keeps the reasoning attached to the work. The bet is simple: a plan you revisit weekly is worth ten plans you present once.

Who it’s for — the core use cases
Industry-agnostic by design — the shape fits any team that runs outcomes on a cadence, whether you’re a startup, an agency, a nonprofit, or a function inside a larger company.
🧭
Quarterly planning
Set 2–4 outcome-led goals, define the measures that prove them, and line up the initiatives that move the needle.
🗓️
The weekly operating rhythm
Run the Monday/Wednesday/Friday huddle off live status — the check-in flow makes updating fast enough to actually happen.
🚀
Running a program at scale
Stand up a multi-team or multi-location program — systems, frameworks, and watched metrics together in one frame.
💡
Capturing & triaging ideas
Catch sparks the moment they appear, then promote the best ones into real initiatives at planning time.
👀
Leadership visibility
See what’s on track, at risk, and shipped — without chasing anyone for a status update.
🤝
Onboarding through turnover
New owners inherit the full Activity Trail and the "why" behind each initiative, so context survives the handover.

Per-initiative status pills, plus overdue and stale chips and a sidebar at-risk marker, surface trouble before the quarter ends.

The design principles underneath
The rules that decided what got built — and, more often, what didn’t.
  • Outcomes over output. A goal is the change you want to create, not a list of tasks. Measures, not activity, define winning.
  • Make the honest path the easy path. If checking in weekly is hard, people won’t — so the check-in flow is the fastest action in the app, not the slowest.
  • Legibility beats completeness. A glanceable at-risk chip is worth more than a perfect report no one reads.
  • Capture is free, commitment is deliberate. Ideas cost nothing to drop in; promoting one to an initiative is the considered step.
  • Forgiving by default. Undo on deletes, safe realtime reconciliation, and clear empty states mean the product never punishes a wrong move.
  • Single-file, no build, no lock-in. The whole app is one HTML file on a static host with an exportable JSON snapshot — easy to run, easy to leave.
Made to feel delightful, not dutiful
A tracker people are made to use becomes a chore. We wanted Compass to feel like a place worth returning to — so the small moments carry warmth, not just data.
  • Finishing should feel like something. Closing a goal sets off a confetti moment with the final score — a small exhale of celebration after a quarter of work.
  • Reward the easy habit. Capturing an idea triggers a tiny spark animation, so the lightweight act of jotting a thought feels good enough to keep doing.
  • Talk like a teammate, not a form. Statuses, empty states, and nudges are written warmly — "This initiative's been quiet — might need a check-in 🧭" lands as a friendly prompt, not a scolding.
  • Soften the rough edges. Smooth expand/collapse, a 5-second undo on deletes, and gentle toasts mean the product feels forgiving and unhurried.
  • Easy on the eyes, day or night. A warm light theme and a cosy dark theme (not steel-blue and clinical) make daily, long-session use comfortable.
What success looks like
The signal that Compass is doing its job.
  • The plan is opened every week, not just at kickoff and post-mortem.
  • Leadership answers "how are we doing?" by looking, not by asking.
  • A slipping initiative gets a conversation weeks before the deadline, not after.
  • When someone rotates out, the next owner reads the trail and keeps moving.
  • At quarter-end the team plans the next leg from real ground truth, not memory.
