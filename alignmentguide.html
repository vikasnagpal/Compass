The Vibe Curator Program

A shared alignment guide.

One playbook for the two people who make it work — the Property Manager who hosts, and the Vibe Curator who brings the property to life. Read it together before the tenure begins.

Reading as…
The big idea
A Vibe Curator is a guest who has chosen to contribute. A Property Manager is a host who has chosen to support them.

Everything in this guide flows from that one line. The program works when both sides hold it. It breaks when either side forgets it.

01Orientation

What this program is

A two-to-three month tenure

The Vibe Curator (VC) Program places travellers with skills in community building, hosting, and content creation at Zostel properties for a 2–3 month tenure. In return for free stay, food, perks, and exposure, VCs bring properties to life — running daily activities, creating content, and turning individual travellers into a community.

This document is meant to be read by both the Property Manager and the Vibe Curator before the tenure begins. It lays out what each side commits to, what each side receives, how to handle the common friction points, and where to go when things need a third party.

How to use this guide

Read it together in your first alignment call — don't skim.

Refer back to it when something feels off. The answer is probably already in here.

When in doubt, default to the big idea above.

02The two roles

Two people, one playbook

The roles mirror each other — each side commits, and each side receives. Use the “Reading as…” switch up top to focus on your side.

Role 1

The Vibe Curator

You are a guest first — one who's chosen to use your skills in community building, hosting, and storytelling to subsidise your travel and grow your craft. You're not staff. You're not a volunteer. You're a guest who contributes.

This is you
What's expected from you
Daily & weekly cadence
  • Run at least 1 activity per day — high-effort (trek, food walk, bonfire) or low-effort (card game, lakeside chill, storytelling).
  • Publish at least 3 social posts per week (reels, posts, or videos) covering life at the property.
  • Update the activity calendar on the Zostel website weekly.
  • Post a weekly community update to HQ on Slack.
Ongoing responsibilities
  • Give every new guest a property tour on arrival.
  • Connect guests to each other — turn solo travellers into a community.
  • Drive review collection — every checkout is a chance for a review.
  • Be professional, follow property rules, and respect the team you live with.
What you receive
During your tenure
  • A bed in the guest dormitory (not the staff dorm).
  • All meals at the property, free.
  • A share of revenue from any paid activities you run.
  • Direct access to guests, community, and the wider Zostel network.
Beyond your tenure
  • Zo Credits based on performance, redeemable for nights at any Zostel.
  • Performance-linked stipend (under finalisation — confirmed at offer).
  • Certificate of completion from Zostel.
  • Portfolio content + priority consideration for future Zostel roles.
Role 2

The Property Manager

You are the host. The VC is not staff — they're a contributor you've agreed to support. Your job is to give them the environment, context, and respect they need to do the work well. When they succeed, your guest experience, reviews, and content all lift with them.

This is you
What's expected from you
Hosting the VC
  • Provide a bed in the guest dormitory — not the staff dorm, no exceptions.
  • Provide all meals at the property, suitable for a guest.
  • Treat the VC with the respect any guest deserves.
  • Introduce the VC to the team, the property, and the local context on day one.
Supporting the work
  • Have a brief weekly check-in with the VC to align on activities and content.
  • Share property handles and the activity calendar with the VC.
  • Back the VC up when they're new — they may need guidance, not criticism.
  • Flag concerns early to the VC directly, and to HQ if unresolved.
What you receive
On the property
  • Stronger guest reviews driven by a more engaged community.
  • A steady stream of content for your property's social handles.
  • Reduced personal workload on guest engagement and activities.
  • A more vibrant property feel that supports retention and word-of-mouth.
Within Zostel
  • Recognition through the PM championship program for managers who run it well.
  • Co-ownership of VC selection going forward — your input shapes who joins.
  • Visibility within the Zostel network as a property running the playbook right.
  • Direct partnership with HQ on the program's design and improvement.
03When it's tricky

When things get tricky

Most VC tenures go smoothly. When they don't, it's almost always one of the three situations below. Each has a clear line and a clear first move.

1

The VC is being treated as housekeeping or ops staff

What's okay
  • VC offers small, optional help as a gesture (e.g., reception during a brief gap).
  • VC pitches in during a one-off crunch by choice.
What's not okay
  • VC is assigned housekeeping, reception, or kitchen shifts.
  • Helping becomes the default expectation, not an occasional gesture.
First move

VC: Name the line politely to the PM.  PM: If unsure where the line is, ask the VC Ops Lead before assigning.

2

The VC is not delivering — missing activities or content

What's okay
  • VC misses a day occasionally and communicates it.
  • VC proactively flags blockers (low guest count, no budget, etc.).
What's not okay
  • VC misses multiple activities in a week without communicating.
  • VC goes unreachable for stretches with no heads-up.
First move

PM: Speak to the VC directly within 24 hours. If unreachable for 24 hours, escalate to the VC Ops Lead.

3

The VC isn't given a guest dorm bed

What's okay
  • VC is in the guest dorm from day one.
  • If the guest dorm is genuinely full on a peak night, the VC is informed in advance and given an upgrade.
What's not okay
  • VC is placed in the staff dorm to preserve a saleable bed.
  • Revenue protection is treated as a reason to downgrade VC accommodation.
First move

VC: Raise it with the PM on day one if the setup is wrong.  PM: Plan VC bed allocation before the tenure starts.

04Check-ins

Check-ins, escalation & contact

Two-week mutual check-in

Two weeks into the tenure, the PM and VC have a structured conversation. Either party can say “this is working” or “I have concerns.” Concerns get raised to the VC Ops Lead, who decides whether to mediate, adjust, or — in rare cases — end the tenure cleanly. This is a planned conversation, not a performance review.

Escalation path

Same path for either party

If something can't be resolved between PM and VC directly, here's the path.

1

Talk it out

Raise it directly with the other party first. Most things resolve here.

2

Wait 24 hours

Give space for response or action. Note what was discussed.

3

Loop in HQ

If unresolved or unreachable, email the VC Ops Lead with context.

4

HQ mediates

The VC Ops Lead steps in within 48 hours to facilitate a resolution.

Ending the tenure early

We expect most VC tenures to run their full 2–3 months. If either party feels it's genuinely not working, the path is: raise it at the two-week check-in or with the VC Ops Lead. Neither party should end the tenure unilaterally — no walking out, no abrupt dismissals. HQ supports a clean exit when one is needed.
