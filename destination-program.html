1 Program Overview

What we're building, and why it matters.

The story of every place, told by someone who actually lives there.

Zostel is about to reach 100 destinations across the map. But travellers don't fall in love with a hostel first — they fall in love with a place. And right now, nobody is telling the story of those places the way a local would.

The Destination Program fixes that. Every destination gets its own social handle, brought to you by a local storyteller, surfacing the hidden gems, the food, the heritage, the art, and the people that make a place worth the trip — the biryani trail of Lucknow, the secret waterfall of Kempty, the spots and legends only locals know.

This is not property marketing. Instead of selling beds, the Destination Program surfaces the story of the places those beds are in.

A property handle says
"Stay with us."
A destination handle says
"Come here, and here's why."
Why it matters

We're not building handles. We're building a micro-ecosystem in every destination.

A living network where local artists, creators, and community collaborate around the handles — with the handles as the anchor that brings them together. We've already seen the shape of this at Pokhara, where travellers arrived and walked out as creators, and a collaboration culture took place on its own.

The hero of this program

Meet the Destination Host.

A local storyteller and content creator who becomes the authentic voice of their region. This is deliberately not the Vibe Curator model — VCs are short-term volunteers tied to a single property; they churn, and chasing them to post is a full-time job. A Destination Host is a local creator with a real stake in their city and their own growth.

For the host

What they get from us

  • A platform and an audience we actively help them build
  • Free stays, Zo Credit, real exposure, access to the Zostel network
  • Milestone rewards as content lands — view targets, follower milestones, streaks
For Zostel

What we get

  • A voice that's genuinely local — and sustainable
  • Not a head-office feed pretending to be from the ground
  • Stories that compound as the host grows
The flywheel

Built to spin on its own.

Each loop makes the next one easier. Our job early on is to push one stage — the host, and a system that enables them. Once that's solid, the rest compounds.

Local host + system → Content gets out → Place gets discovered → Artists & community join → Richer stories → Brand & bookings rise ↺ More & better hosts join

The operating model

Who owns what — and how it shifts.

The content owner isn't fixed; it shifts as the program matures. This is what lets us be authentic (local hosts own the voice) without being fragile (no handle depends on unproven volunteers).

Capture Create & Edit Publish HQ's role
Phase 1 · Crawl Host + VC HQ HQ Operate the handles
Phase 2 · Walk Host + VC Host (HQ supports) Host or HQ Coach + backstop
Phase 3 · Run Host + Collaborators + VC Host Hosts Ideas, VC content feed, tools, backstop
Host Graduation Criteria
A host moves from HQ-produced to host-led once they sustain ~4–5 weeks of cadence without chasing, content clears the quality bar, and early audience traction shows. Until they graduate, HQ produces — so a flaky host never takes a handle dark.

Phase 1 exit gate

Don't start Phase 2 until all of this is true.

Hard gate

Phase 1 exit checklist

Every item below must be green before we begin handoff to hosts.

  • 10 handles live, claimed, Meta-connected.
  • >80% posting cadence hit rate sustained for 3+ weeks without chasing.
  • All four systems live and in daily use (Sys-1 → Sys-4).
  • All six frameworks documented and applied (FW-1 → FW-6).
  • All five dashboards live with real data (DB-1 → DB-5).
  • 10 hosts retained through the phase (churn test).
  • At least 2–3 hosts showing graduation readiness: consistent capture, quality, early traction.
  • ≥1 local collaboration per destination initiated.
  • Reward / milestone framework has paid out at least once cleanly.

Phase 2 · Validation

25–30 handles · Days 61–150

The handoff begins. Graduated hosts take over creation and posting for their destinations; HQ shifts from operator to coach + backstop. Milestone rewards become the primary retention lever now that hosts own output. We scale the systems and frameworks built in Phase 1 rather than building new ones. Watch DB-3 (Host health) closely — this phase proves whether the perks-only model retains.

Phase 3 · Scale

100% coverage · Days 150+

Host-led by default. HQ's job narrows to feeding ideas and VC-captured content, maintaining tools, paying milestones, and backstopping the occasional gap. Cross-platform (LinkedIn / X) opens for standout hosts via the creator playbook (FW-5). The micro-ecosystem — artists, collaborators, local legends — becomes the main content engine, and the flywheel spins with minimal push.
